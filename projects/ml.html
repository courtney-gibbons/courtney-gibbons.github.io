Machine Learning Projects

My machine learning projects vary from implementing FC nets and CNNs from scratch in Numpy to producing a heartrate waveform from a face video within 1 BPM accuracy in PyTorch!

Class Project

rPPG

Remote photoplethysmography (rPPG) is a contact-free method for measuring a patient's heart rate and pulse signal using just a video of the patient's face. As part of an honors seminar at UCLA in partnership with Google and the Visual Machines Group, I trained two models for this task. I retrained PhysNet's model and compared it to my own 3D CNN model, obtaining MAEs of 1.02 and 2.24 BPM, respectively. These models were ranked #1 and #3 in the class.

Class Project

Neural Networks in Numpy

I hand-calculated back propagation to achieve loss and gradient expressions, vectorized these expressions, coded them in Numpy using broadcasting, and hand-tuned hyperparameters to classify CIFAR-10. I implemented this for affine layers, activation layers, convolutional layers, maxpool layers, the softmax classifier, gradient descent, optimizers, batchnorm, and dropout! Because of this, I have an foundational understanding of neural networks and deep learning in terms of gradients, linear algebra, and implementation.

Facial Recognition

I coded a headless Raspberry Pi and camera module in Python to 1) detect and save images of a user's face to a directory with their name, 2) train a machine learning model on the images of the desired user's face versus the faces of other users, and 3) use the machine learning model to recognize the desired user's face among other faces.
